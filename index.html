My Projects

E&J Gallo Winery - MEng Capstone Project
November 2023 - May 2024 | Modesto, CA

The team developed a k-means clustering model that assigns customers to clusters based on their historical purchasing habits to help the marketing managers of some of E&J Gallo Winery's best-selling brands determine how to advertise to their customers for effective customer retention.

The team focused on the customer characteristics of the top 3 brands-- Barefoot, High Noon and New Amsterdam-- in order to show that the model is able to output unique, "personalized" customer cluster assignments to cater to each brand. We will be presenting our findings to senior members of the E&J Gallo Winery team on May 7th.

Big Red Sports Network - Baseball Analytics Team
October 2023 - May 2024 | Ithaca, NY

The team scraped data from Synergy and Trackman to write scouting reports on opponent batters, highlighting their tendencies on when they swing at and how often they put the ball in play against certain pitch types, pitch locations and counts.

I volunteered to put in extra work and created a pitcher dashboard that describes how often opponent pitchers utilize certain pitch types against batter handedness and first pitch/2 strike situations; our work has helped the Cornell baseball team earn their first Ivy League Tournament appearance since 2012.

King County Metro - Senior Capstone Design
January 2023 - June 2023 | Seattle, WA

The team developed an ML model powered by random forest and neural networks to improve on King County Metro's existing linear system of predicting bus arrival times, going beyond simple bus location tracking by considering a wide variety of factors such as traffic conditions, speed, bus line and number of passengers expected to load and unload at the next stop.

We also connected our model to King County Metro's official API that outputs live statistics of every bus currently running in the King County Metro system. Training on historical bus arrival data has resulted in our model being able to predict arrivals with a mean error of 2 minutes.
